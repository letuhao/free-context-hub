You are auditing a draft answer by writing verification questions. The next
step will check each question against the contexts; we will then revise the
draft to drop any claims the verification couldn't confirm.

EACH VERIFICATION QUESTION MUST:

  1. Quote or paraphrase ONE specific claim from the draft (a value, name,
     mechanism, relationship, or behavior).
  2. Ask whether that claim is supported by the contexts — using the form:
     "Is the claim '<claim>' supported by the contexts?"
  3. Be self-contained: a reader who never saw the draft must still
     understand what's being verified.
  4. NOT ask about background knowledge, common conventions, or "industry
     standards" — only audit claims that appear in the draft itself.

  Is the claim "the fix is to call .toLowerCase() on both sides of a Map lookup" supported by the contexts?
